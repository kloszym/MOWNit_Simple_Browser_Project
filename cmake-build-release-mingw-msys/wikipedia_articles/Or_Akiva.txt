Or Akiva (Hebrew: אור עקיבא, lit. 'Light of Akiva') is a city located in the Haifa District of Israel, on the country's coastal plain. It is located inland from the ancient port city of Caesarea and the Mediterranean Sea, and to the north of the city of Hadera. It is 39 kilometres (24 miles) south of Haifa and 48 km (30 mi) north of Tel Aviv. In 2022 it had a population of 20,874.


== History ==

Or Akiva was founded in the early 1950s as a ma’abara (transit camp) for new immigrants, the majority hailing from Morocco. It was built on the land of the depopulated Palestinian village Barrat Qisarya. In the 1990s, new immigrants from the former Soviet Union began to settle there, which led to an upswing in building and development.


== Demographics ==

According to the Israel Central Bureau of Statistics (CBS), at the end of 2005 the city had a total population of 15,800, making it is the least-populous city in Israel. According to CBS, in 2001 the ethnic makeup of the city was 99.3% Jewish and other non-Arab, with no significant Arab population. There were 7,400 males and 7,900 females.
