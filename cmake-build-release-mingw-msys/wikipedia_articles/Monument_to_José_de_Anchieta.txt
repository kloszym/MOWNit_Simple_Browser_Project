== Description ==
The monumental sculpture is dedicated to Joseph of Anchieta, who was born in La Laguna in 1534, before going to Brazil as a missionary in 1553. The bronze sculpture is approximately 5 metres (16 ft) tall, and is on a 1 metre (3.3 ft) concrete pedestal. The statue's position is described as "walking towards the sea and looking at La Laguna". It is one of La Laguna's emblematic symbols.


== History ==
The creation of the monument was promoted by a pro-monument commission of the Ayuntamiento of La Laguna, at the prompting of Celso Ferreira da Cunha in the late 1950s.
The monument was created by Bruno Giorgi, and was funded by the Federal government of Brazil. Constructed in Brazil, it was moved to Tenerife in 1960 on the ocean liner Cabo de San Vicente.
