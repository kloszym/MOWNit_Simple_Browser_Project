Bellview is a census-designated place (CDP) in Escambia County, Florida, United States. It is considered to be a community within Pensacola. The population was 25,541 at the 2020 census, up from 23,355 at the 2010 census. It is part of the Pensacola–Ferry Pass–Brent, Florida Metropolitan Statistical Area.


== Geography ==
Bellview is
30°27′40″N 87°18′3″W (30.461213, -87.300822), 7 miles (11 km) northwest of downtown Pensacola. U.S. Route 90, the Mobile Highway, is the main route through the CDP.
According to the United States Census Bureau, the CDP has a total area of 11.8 square miles (30.5 km2), of which 11.7 square miles (30.2 km2) is land and 0.15 square miles (0.4 km2), or 1.16%, is water.


== Demographics ==

As of the census of 2000, there were 21,201 people, 8,108 households, and 5,951 families residing in the CDP.  The population density was 1,793.3 inhabitants per square mile (692.4/km2).  There were 8,673 housing units at an average density of 733.6 per square mile (283.2/km2).  The racial makeup of the CDP was 80.66% White, 11.69% African American, 1.08% Native American, 3.28% Asian, 0.16% Pacific Islander, 0.67% from other races, and 2.46% from two or more races.
