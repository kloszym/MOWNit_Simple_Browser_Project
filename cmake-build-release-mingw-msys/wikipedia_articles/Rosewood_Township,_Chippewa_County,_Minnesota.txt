Rosewood Township is a township in Chippewa County, Minnesota, United States. The population was 303 at the year 2000 census.


== History ==
Rosewood Township was organized in 1871, and named after Rosewood, Ohio, the former home of a share of early settlers.


== Geography ==
According to the United States Census Bureau, the township has a total area of 35.8 square miles (92.6 km2), of which 35.7 square miles (92.4 km2)  is land and 0.1 square mile (0.2 km2)  (0.22%) is water.


== Demographics ==
As of the census of 2000, there were 303 people, 115 households, and 92 families residing in the township.  The population density was 8.5 people per square mile (3.3/km2).  There were 123 housing units at an average density of 3.4/sq mi (1.3/km2).  The racial makeup of the township was 98.68% White, 0.66% African American, 0.33% Asian, 0.33% from other races.
