Kamilo Mašek (July 11, 1831 – June 29, 1859) was a music composer from the Austrian Empire. Mašek was a son of Czech-born Kašpar Mašek. Mašek had introduced more romantic influence into Slovenian music. His compositions include various sacred works.


== Works ==
Overture to the melodrama Judith (1854), for orchestra
Polkas for wind orchestra
Divertimento for two violas, two cellos and double bass (1854)
Fantasy for piano and melofon
Choral Music: The Lake
Songs, a wreath of songs on texts by France Prešeren
