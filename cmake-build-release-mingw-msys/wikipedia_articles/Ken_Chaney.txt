== Life and work ==
Chaney grew up in Edmonton, led dance bands as a teenager and moved first to Detroit, then to Chicago in the early 1960s. In 1965, he took part in the first sessions of the Association for the Advancement of Creative Musicians and in 1967, worked with Muhal Richard Abrams and Jodie Christian. During this time, he began his work as a music teacher and taught music theory. With the soul-jazz trio Young-Holt Unlimited around Redd Holt, he recorded the title Soulful Strut, an instrumental version of the song "Am I the Same Girl", which sold over a million copies and reached #3 on the Billboard Hot 100 charts. . After the dissolution of Young-Holt Unlimited in the early 1970s, Chaney founded the post-hard bop sextet The Awakening, which played in the style of Art Blakey's Jazz Messengers, supplemented by the electric piano and musical influences from Woody Shaw and Pharoah Sanders.
