Tompkins Township is a civil township of Jackson County in the U.S. state of Michigan. The population was 2,618 at the 2020 census.


== Geography ==
According to the United States Census Bureau, the township has a total area of 36.36 square miles (94.17 km2), of which 36.01 square miles (93.27 km2) is land and 0.35 square miles (0.91 km2) (0.96%) is water.
Tompkins Township is in northwestern Jackson County and is bordered to the north by Ingham County. State highway M-50 crosses the township, leading southeast 14 miles (23 km) to Jackson, the county seat, and northwest 13 miles (21 km) to Eaton Rapids.  The Grand River crosses the northeastern portion of the township.


== Demographics ==
As of the census of 2000, there were 2,758 people, 985 households, and 781 families residing in the township.  The population density was 76.4 inhabitants per square mile (29.5/km2).  There were 1,032 housing units at an average density of 28.6 per square mile (11.0/km2).  The racial makeup of the township was 96.74% White, 0.51% African American, 0.18% Native American, 0.15% Asian, 0.80% from other races, and 1.63% from two or more races.
