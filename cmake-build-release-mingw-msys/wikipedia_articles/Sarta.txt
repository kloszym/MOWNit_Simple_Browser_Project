Sarta (Arabic: سرطه) is a Palestinian town in the Salfit Governorate in the northern West Bank, 22 kilometers southwest of Nablus. According to the Palestinian Central Bureau of Statistics, it had a population of approximately 3,382 in 2017.


== Location ==
Sarta is bordered by Haris  to the east, Bruqin  to the south, Biddya to the west, and Qarawat Bani Hassan  to the north.


== History ==
Sarta is on an ancient site, where  cisterns and columbariums carved into rock have been found.  Sherds from Iron Age II and  Persian eras have been found, but were possibly washed down from a nearby higher Tell.  Sherds from Byzantine/Early Umayyad and Crusader/Ayyubid  occupations can be suggested according to the finds of sherds at Sarta, and according to finds at the site of the nearby sheikh tomb.
Yakut mentions "Suratah", as being in "a village in Jabal Nabulus".  It has been suggested that this was  Sarta.
